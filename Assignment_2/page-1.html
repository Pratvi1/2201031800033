civil war

The American Civil War was a war between the Union (Northern US States) and the Confederacy (Southern US States) that lasted from 1861 to 1865

The war was fought over disagreements about slavery, states' rights, the election of Abraham Lincoln, and the economy. 

The war left the South economically devastated and resulted in the criminalization of slavery in the United States. Confederate General Lee surrendered to U.S. General Grant in the spring of 1865, officially ending the war.
