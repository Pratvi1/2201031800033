cold war history

The Cold War was a period of conflict between the United States and the Soviet Union that lasted from the end of World War II until 1991. The two superpowers competed for dominance in politics, economics, science, and technology, but never directly fought each other. 

Instead, they supported opposing sides in proxy wars and increased their military capabilities. 

The Cold War ended in 1991 when the Soviet Union dissolved. The Soviet Union's new leader, Mikhael Gorbachev, proposed reforms to stimulate the communist economy and relaxed restrictions on freedom of expression. The Berlin Wall came down in 1989 and 1990, and free elections ousted Communist regimes in eastern Europe.
