political history

  • 1947: India gained independence from British rule and became a sovereign state within the British Commonwealth. The Indian National Congress Party ('Congress') dominated the government.
  • 1951: India held its first general election, which the Indian National Congress won. The party continued to dominate elections until 1977, when a non-Congress government was formed for the first time.
  • 1956: The second five-year plan was presented, focusing on rapid industrialization.
  • 1959: The Dalai Lama escaped to India.
  • 1967: Farmers harvested the first crop of high-yielding wheat, which laid the foundation of the Green Revolution.
  • 1984: The Bhopal Gas Tragedy killed more than 3,500 people and injured 500,000.
