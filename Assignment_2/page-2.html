kashmir’s history

  • Hindu dynasties: From the beginning until 1346, Kashmir was ruled by Hindu dynasties.
  • Muslim rule: In 1346, Kashmir came under Muslim rule, which lasted nearly five centuries.
  • Mughal rule: In 1586, Mughal emperor Akbar the Great added Kashmir to his Kabul Subah. The region was part of the Mughal Empire from 1586 to 1751.
  • Durrani Empire: In 1752, the Afghan Durrani Empire took control of Kashmir. The region was part of the Afghan Durrani Empire from 1751 to 1820.
  • Sikh rule: In 1819, Kashmir was annexed to the Sikh kingdom of the Punjab. In 1846, Kashmir was annexed to the Dogra kingdom of Jammu.
  • Creation of the princely state of Kashmir: In 1846, the princely state of Kashmir was created.
  • Partition: In 1949, Kashmir was partitioned between India and Pakistan, with a ceasefire line agreed.
  • War: In 1947-8, war broke out between India and Pakistan over the region.
  • Second Indo-Pakistan war: In 1965, the second Indo-Pakistan war over Kashmir ended.
